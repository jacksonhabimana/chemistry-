Acid-Base Theories

Arrhenius Theory (1884)

The earliest theory, applicable only to aqueous solutions:

Arrhenius Acid A substance that dissociates in water to produce HвҒә ions (protons). e.g. HCl вҶ’ HвҒә + ClвҒ»; HвӮӮSOвӮ„ вҶ’ 2HвҒә + SOвӮ„ВІвҒ»
Arrhenius Base A substance that dissociates in water to produce OHвҒ» ions. e.g. NaOH вҶ’ NaвҒә + OHвҒ»; Ca(OH)вӮӮ вҶ’ CaВІвҒә + 2OHвҒ»

Limitations: Cannot explain why NHвӮғ is a base (no OHвҒ» in its formula), or acid-base reactions in non-aqueous solvents.

BrГёnsted-Lowry Theory (1923)

A broader theory based on proton (HвҒә) transfer:

BrГёnsted-Lowry Acid A proton (HвҒә) donor вҖ” loses a proton to another species.
BrГёnsted-Lowry Base A proton (HвҒә) acceptor вҖ” gains a proton from an acid.

In every BrГёnsted-Lowry acid-base reaction, a proton is transferred. There is always a conjugate acid-base pair:

HCl + HвӮӮO вҶ’ HвӮғOвҒә + ClвҒ» acidвӮҒ + baseвӮӮ вҶ’ acidвӮӮ + baseвӮҒ (HCl/ClвҒ» is conjugate pair 1; HвӮғOвҒә/HвӮӮO is conjugate pair 2) NHвӮғ + HвӮӮO вҮҢ NHвӮ„вҒә + OHвҒ» baseвӮҒ + acidвӮӮ вҶ’ acidвӮҒ + baseвӮӮ (NHвӮ„вҒә/NHвӮғ is conjugate pair 1; HвӮӮO/OHвҒ» is conjugate pair 2)
рҹ’Ў
Conjugate acid-base pairs differ by ONE proton (HвҒә) HCl and ClвҒ» are a conjugate pair (differ by HвҒә). NHвӮ„вҒә and NHвӮғ are a conjugate pair. HвӮӮO can act as either acid (donates HвҒә вҶ’ OHвҒ») or base (accepts HвҒә вҶ’ HвӮғOвҒә) вҖ” water is amphiprotic.

Lewis Theory (1923)

The most general theory вҖ” based on electron pair donation:

Lewis Acid An electron pair acceptor вҖ” accepts a lone pair of electrons to form a coordinate bond.
Lewis Base An electron pair donor вҖ” donates a lone pair to a Lewis acid.
BFвӮғ + NHвӮғ вҶ’ FвӮғBвҶҗNHвӮғ (BFвӮғ = Lewis acid, has empty p orbital; NHвӮғ = Lewis base) AlClвӮғ + ClвҒ» вҶ’ AlClвӮ„вҒ» (AlClвӮғ = Lewis acid; ClвҒ» = Lewis base) FeВівҒә + 6HвӮӮO вҶ’ [Fe(HвӮӮO)вӮҶ]ВівҒә (FeВівҒә = Lewis acid; HвӮӮO = Lewis base) CuВІвҒә + 4NHвӮғ вҶ’ [Cu(NHвӮғ)вӮ„]ВІвҒә (CuВІвҒә = Lewis acid; NHвӮғ = Lewis base)

Lewis theory explains all BrГёnsted-Lowry reactions (HвҒә is a Lewis acid вҖ” accepts lone pair from a base) and extends to reactions without HвҒә transfer, including complex ion formation and many organic reactions.

Strong and Weak Acids and Bases

Strong Acids

A strong acid is one that is completely (fully) dissociated in aqueous solution вҖ” all molecules ionise:

HCl(aq) вҶ’ HвҒә(aq) + ClвҒ»(aq) 100% ionised HNOвӮғ(aq) вҶ’ HвҒә(aq) + NOвӮғвҒ»(aq) 100% ionised HпҝҪпҝҪSOвӮ„(aq) вҶ’ 2HвҒә(aq) + SOвӮ„ВІвҒ»(aq) 100% first ionisation (2nd: KaвӮӮ = 0.012)

The six common strong acids: HCl, HBr, HI, HNOвӮғ, HвӮӮSOвӮ„, HClOвӮ„. Strong acids give pH very close to вҲ’log[acid].

Weak Acids

A weak acid is partially dissociated in aqueous solution вҖ” only a small fraction of molecules ionise. The dissociation is an equilibrium:

CHвӮғCOOH(aq) вҮҢ HвҒә(aq) + CHвӮғCOOвҒ»(aq) Ka = 1.8Г—10вҒ»вҒө mol dmвҒ»Ві HCN(aq) вҮҢ HвҒә(aq) + CNвҒ»(aq) Ka = 4.9Г—10вҒ»В№вҒ° mol dmвҒ»Ві HпҝҪпҝҪCOвӮғ(aq) вҮҢ HвҒә(aq) + HCOвӮғвҒ»(aq) KaвӮҒ = 4.3Г—10вҒ»вҒ· mol dmвҒ»Ві HпҝҪпҝҪPOвӮ„(aq) вҮҢ HвҒә(aq) + HвӮӮPOвӮ„вҒ»(aq) KaвӮҒ = 7.5Г—10вҒ»Ві mol dmвҒ»Ві

Examples of weak acids: CHвӮғCOOH (ethanoic/acetic), HCN, HвӮӮCOвӮғ, HвӮӮSOвӮғ, HвӮғPOвӮ„, HF, organic carboxylic acids. Most are molecular organic acids.

Strong and Weak Bases

Strong bases: completely ionised in water вҖ” Group 1 hydroxides (NaOH, KOH, LiOH) and Ca(OH)вӮӮ (sparingly soluble but fully ionised when dissolved).

NaOH(aq) вҶ’ NaвҒә(aq) + OHвҒ»(aq) 100% ionised

Weak bases: partially ionised вҖ” accept proton from water in a reversible equilibrium:

NHвӮғ(aq) + HвӮӮO(l) вҮҢ NHвӮ„вҒә(aq) + OHвҒ»(aq) Kb = 1.8Г—10вҒ»вҒө mol dmвҒ»Ві CHвӮғNHвӮӮ(aq) + HвӮӮO вҮҢ CHвӮғNHвӮғвҒә(aq) + OHвҒ»(aq) (methylamine, slightly stronger base than NHвӮғ)
рҹ§ 
Strong vs weak: strength вү  concentration! A "concentrated weak acid" (e.g. concentrated ethanoic acid) is NOT the same as a strong acid. Strength refers to the degree of ionisation; concentration refers to amount of acid per unit volume. 0.1 mol dmвҒ»Ві HCl is a dilute strong acid. 10 mol dmвҒ»Ві CHвӮғCOOH is a concentrated weak acid. Dilute HCl has a much lower pH than concentrated ethanoic acid.

pH, pOH, and Calculations

The pH Scale

pH = вҲ’logвӮҒвӮҖ[HвҒә] (or вҲ’logвӮҒвӮҖ[HвӮғOвҒә]) [HвҒә] = 10^(вҲ’pH) pOH = вҲ’logвӮҒвӮҖ[OHвҒ»] pH + pOH = 14 (at 25В°C) The ionic product of water: Kw = [HвҒә][OHвҒ»] = 1.0Г—10вҒ»В№вҒҙ molВІ dmвҒ»вҒ¶ at 25В°C Therefore: pKw = 14 = pH + pOH

pH Calculations

Strong Acid

HCl fully ionises вҶ’ [HвҒә] = [HCl] 0.01 mol dmвҒ»Ві HCl: [HвҒә] = 0.01 = 10вҒ»ВІ вҶ’ pH = 2 HпҝҪпҝҪSOвӮ„ (dilute, both protons): [HвҒә] = 2Г—[HвӮӮSOвӮ„] 0.05 mol dmвҒ»Ві HвӮӮSOвӮ„: [HвҒә] = 0.10 вҶ’ pH = 1

Strong Base

NaOH fully ionises вҶ’ [OHвҒ»] = [NaOH] 0.01 mol dmвҒ»Ві NaOH: [OHвҒ»] = 0.01 вҶ’ pOH = 2 вҶ’ pH = 14 вҲ’ 2 = 12

Weak Acid (using Ka)

For HA вҮҢ HвҒә + AвҒ»: Ka = [HвҒә][AвҒ»] / [HA] Assume [HвҒә] = [AвҒ»] = x and [HA] вүҲ c (initial conc., valid when Ka << c) Then: Ka = xВІ / c вҶ’ x = вҲҡ(Ka Г— c) вҶ’ [HвҒә] = вҲҡ(Ka Г— c) Example: 0.10 mol dmвҒ»Ві CHвӮғCOOH, Ka = 1.8Г—10вҒ»вҒө [HвҒә] = вҲҡ(1.8Г—10вҒ»вҒө Г— 0.10) = вҲҡ(1.8Г—10вҒ»вҒ¶) = 1.34Г—10вҒ»Ві mol dmвҒ»Ві pH = вҲ’log(1.34Г—10вҒ»Ві) = 2.87

Buffer Solutions

Definition вҖ” Buffer Solution A solution that resists significant changes in pH when small amounts of acid or alkali are added, or when the solution is diluted. It typically consists of a weak acid and its conjugate base (or a weak base and its conjugate acid) in comparable amounts.

Acidic Buffer вҖ” Weak Acid + Conjugate Base Salt

Example: CHвӮғCOOH / CHвӮғCOONa (ethanoic acid / sodium ethanoate). pH вүҲ 4вҖ“6.

How it works:

  • When HвҒә added: CHвӮғCOOвҒ»(aq) + HвҒә(aq) вҶ’ CHвӮғCOOH(aq). The conjugate base "mops up" added HвҒә вҶ’ pH barely changes.
  • When OHвҒ» added: CHвӮғCOOH(aq) + OHвҒ»(aq) вҶ’ CHвӮғCOOвҒ»(aq) + HвӮӮO. The weak acid "neutralises" added OHвҒ» вҶ’ pH barely changes.

Buffer pH (Henderson-Hasselbalch equation):

pH = pKa + log([AвҒ»] / [HA]) = pKa + log([salt] / [acid]) At equal concentrations of acid and salt: pH = pKa (simplest case)
рҹ’Ў
A buffer is most effective when [HA] вүҲ [AвҒ»] Equal amounts of weak acid and its conjugate base вҶ’ pH = pKa. Buffer capacity is greatest at this point. Buffer is generally effective within Вұ1 pH unit of pKa.

Basic Buffer вҖ” Weak Base + Conjugate Acid Salt

Example: NHвӮғ / NHвӮ„Cl (ammonia / ammonium chloride). pH вүҲ 8вҖ“10.

When HвҒә added: NHвӮғ + HвҒә вҶ’ NHвӮ„вҒә (NHвӮғ mops up HвҒә) When OHвҒ» added: NHвӮ„вҒә + OHвҒ» вҶ’ NHвӮғ + HвӮӮO (NHвӮ„вҒә mops up OHвҒ») pH = 14 вҲ’ pKb вҲ’ log([NHвӮ„вҒә]/[NHвӮғ]) or use pKa of NHвӮ„вҒә (9.25) in Henderson-Hasselbalch
Worked Example 16.3 вҖ” Buffer pH Calculation

Calculate the pH of a buffer made from 0.20 mol dmвҒ»Ві CHвӮғCOOH and 0.40 mol dmвҒ»Ві CHвӮғCOONa. (Ka = 1.7Г—10вҒ»вҒө)

1

pH = pKa + log([AвҒ»]/[HA]) = 4.77 + log(0.40/0.20) = 4.77 + log(2) = 4.77 + 0.30 = 5.07

Because [AвҒ»] > [HA], pH > pKa (as expected вҖ” more base than acid).

Biological Importance of Buffers

  • Blood buffer (pH 7.35вҖ“7.45): HвӮӮCOвӮғ/HCOвӮғвҒ» system (carbonate buffer). COвӮӮ + HвӮӮO вҮҢ HвӮӮCOвӮғ вҮҢ HвҒә + HCOвӮғвҒ». Lungs regulate COвӮӮ (breathing); kidneys regulate HCOвӮғвҒ». pH outside 7.0вҖ“7.8 is fatal.
  • Intracellular buffer: HвӮӮPOвӮ„вҒ»/HPOвӮ„ВІвҒ» (pKa = 7.2) вҖ” important inside cells
  • Protein buffers: amino acid side chains (histidine imidazole, pKa вүҲ 6.0) buffer inside cells
  • Industrial: buffer solutions used in fermentation, pharmaceuticals, food processing, photography

Definition вҖ” Buffer Solution A solution that resists changes in pH when small amounts of acid or base are added. It maintains a nearly constant pH. Buffers contain a weak acid and its conjugate base (or a weak base and its conjugate acid) in comparable concentrations.

An acidic buffer contains a weak acid (HA) and its conjugate base (AвҒ»), typically as its sodium salt NaA:

Example: CHвӮғCOOH / CHвӮғCOOвҒ»NaвҒә buffer (pKa = 4.75; effective range pH 3.75вҖ“5.75) When acid (HвҒә) is added: CHвӮғCOOвҒ» + HвҒә вҶ’ CHвӮғCOOH (base component neutralises added HвҒә вҶ’ pH barely changes) When base (OHвҒ») is added: CHвӮғCOOH + OHвҒ» вҶ’ CHвӮғCOOвҒ» + HвӮӮO (acid component neutralises added OHвҒ» вҶ’ pH barely changes)
