Trends down Group 1

Melting point decreases (weaker metallic bonds — larger atoms, less charge density). Density generally increases. Atomic radius increases. IE₁ decreases. Reactivity increases.

6.3

Group 1 Compounds

Thermal stability of carbonates and nitrates

Group 1 carbonates: all stable to heat (except Li₂CO₃ which decomposes). Group 1 nitrates: decompose to nitrite + oxygen (Li nitrate → oxide + NO₂ + O₂).
MNO₃ → MNO₂ + ½O₂ (Na, K, Rb, Cs)  |  4LiNO₃ → 2Li₂O + 4NO₂ + O₂ (Li behaves like Mg — diagonal relationship)

6.4

Flame Tests

When metal ions are heated in a flame, electrons absorb energy, become excited, and fall back emitting light of specific wavelengths (colours).

Metal ionFlame colour
Li⁺Crimson red
Na⁺Yellow-orange
K⁺Lilac/violet
Ca²⁺Brick red
Sr²⁺Crimson
Ba²⁺Green

Procedure: clean platinum/nichrome wire with HCl, dip in sample, hold in blue flame, observe colour through cobalt blue glass (to filter Na yellow).

6.5

Hydrogen — Chemistry and Uses

Hydrogen can be produced from Group 1 metal reactions with water. Industrial production: steam reforming of methane (CH₄ + H₂O → CO + 3H₂).

PropertyValue

Reaction with halogensH₂ + Cl₂ → 2HCl
6.6

The Diagonal Relationship — Li and Mg

Why Li resembles Mg more than Na

Li and Mg have similar charge/radius ratios (charge densities). Both: form oxide (not peroxide) with O₂; nitrate decomposes to oxide + NO₂ + O₂; carbonate decomposes on heating; form ionic nitrides (Li₃N, Mg₃N₂); chlorides are covalent in nature (soluble in organic solvents).
