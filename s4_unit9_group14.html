Overview of Group 14

Group 14 (also called Group IVA or Group IVB) contains Carbon (C), Silicon (Si), Germanium (Ge), Tin (Sn), and Lead (Pb). All have the outer electron configuration nsВІnpВІ giving 4 valence electrons.

Carbon and Its Allotropes

Definition вЂ” Allotropy The existence of an element in two or more different physical forms in the same physical state, with different arrangements of atoms. Each form is called an allotrope.

Diamond

In diamond, each carbon atom is spВі hybridised and bonded to four other carbon atoms in a regular tetrahedral arrangement, forming a giant covalent (macromolecular) structure. Bond angle = 109.5В°.

Properties of Diamond:

  • Extremely hard (hardest natural substance) вЂ” all 4 bonds must be broken to cleave it
  • Very high melting point (~3550В°C) вЂ” strong covalent bonds throughout
  • Does not conduct electricity вЂ” all 4 valence electrons used in bonding, no free electrons
  • Transparent to visible light вЂ” wide band gap
  • Good thermal conductor вЂ” vibrations transfer efficiently through the rigid lattice

Graphite

In graphite, each carbon atom is spВІ hybridised and bonded to three other carbon atoms in a flat hexagonal layer. Bond angle = 120В°. The unhybridised p orbital on each carbon overlaps sideways to form a delocalised ПЂ system extending across each layer.

Properties of Graphite:

  • Conducts electricity вЂ” each C uses only 3 of its 4 valence electrons in Пѓ bonds; the 4th forms the ПЂ system with delocalised electrons free to carry charge
  • Soft and slippery (lubricant) вЂ” layers held by weak van der Waals forces; layers slide over each other easily
  • High melting point within layers (strong covalent bonds), but cleaves between layers
  • Black/dark grey colour вЂ” absorbs all visible light (many ПЂ transitions)
  • Used in pencils, electrodes, lubricants, crucibles

Discovered in 1985. Cв‚†в‚Ђ consists of 60 carbon atoms arranged in 12 pentagons and 20 hexagons вЂ” resembling a football (soccer ball). Each carbon is spВІ hybridised and bonded to 3 others. The molecule is approximately spherical with diameter ~0.7 nm.

Graphene

A single layer of graphite вЂ” one atom thick. It is the thinnest and one of the strongest materials known. Graphene conducts electricity and heat exceptionally well and is transparent (~97.7% of visible light passes through). It is considered a 2D material.

+2 and +4 Oxidation States вЂ” The Inert Pair Effect

The Inert Pair Effect

Going down Group 14 (and Groups 13, 15), the +2 oxidation state becomes increasingly stable relative to +4. This is called the inert pair effect.

Explanation: The outermost s electrons (nsВІ) are called the "inert pair." For heavier elements (Sn, Pb), the nsВІ electrons are in an orbital that has penetrated through many inner electron shells and the relativistic effects increase. The energy required to unpair and use these 2s electrons for bonding is not recovered by the additional bond energies formed. As a result:

  • For Carbon and Silicon: +4 is the dominant stable state (both 2p electrons easily promoted)
  • For Germanium: +4 dominant, but +2 possible (GeO, GeClв‚‚ exist)
  • For Tin: both +2 and +4 are stable (SnClв‚‚ and SnClв‚„ both common)
  • For Lead: +2 is the dominant stable state; Pb(IV) compounds are strong oxidising agents (unstable)

Elements in the second row of the periodic table (Period 2) often show similarities with the element to the lower right (diagonal) in Period 3. This happens because charge density (charge/radius) is similar diagonally.
