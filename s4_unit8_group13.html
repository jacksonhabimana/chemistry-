The Inert Pair Effect

Down Group 13, the +1 oxidation state becomes more stable relative to +3. The 6s² electrons in Tl are reluctant to be used in bonding (inert pair) — due to relativistic effects and poor shielding by d and f electrons.

8.2

Extraction of Aluminium

Hall-Héroult Process

Al is extracted by electrolysis of molten Al₂O₃ (alumina) dissolved in cryolite (Na₃AlF₆) to lower the melting point from 2072°C to ~960°C.

ElectrodeReactionProduct
Cathode (−)Al³⁺ + 3e⁻ → AlLiquid aluminium (sinks to bottom)
Anode (+) [carbon]2O²⁻ → O₂ + 4e⁻O₂ gas — reacts with carbon anode forming CO₂; anodes must be replaced regularly

Ore: bauxite (Al₂O₃·2H₂O) is first purified by the Bayer process to remove iron oxide impurities.

8.3

Amphoteric Nature of Aluminium

Definition

An amphoteric substance can react with both acids AND alkalis. Al, Al₂O₃, and Al(OH)₃ are all amphoteric.

Reaction typeEquationProducts
Al with dilute HCl2Al + 6HCl → 2AlCl₃ + 3H₂Aluminium chloride + hydrogen
Al with NaOH(aq)2Al + 2NaOH + 2H₂O → 2NaAlO₂ + 3H₂Sodium aluminate + hydrogen
Al₂O₃ with HClAl₂O₃ + 6HCl → 2AlCl₃ + 3H₂OActs as base
Al₂O₃ with NaOHAl₂O₃ + 2NaOH → 2NaAlO₂ + H₂OActs as acid
Al(OH)₃ with HClAl(OH)₃ + 3HCl → AlCl₃ + 3H₂OActs as base
Al(OH)₃ with NaOHAl(OH)₃ + NaOH → NaAlO₂ + 2H₂OActs as acid
8.4

Aluminium Chloride

Anhydrous vs Hydrated AlCl₃

Anhydrous AlCl₃ is covalent (Al³⁺ is small and highly charged — polarises Cl⁻ strongly → covalent character by Fajans' rules). It exists as a dimer Al₂Cl₆ in gas phase — each Al accepts a lone pair from Cl via dative bond.

Hydrated AlCl₃·6H₂O is ionic — [Al(H₂O)₆]³⁺ ions form. Dissolves in water to give acidic solution (Al³⁺ hydrolyses water).

BondingPredominantly covalentIonic

8.6

The Protective Oxide Layer

Aluminium is more reactive than iron (higher in reactivity series), yet it does not corrode in air. This is because it rapidly forms a thin, tough, adherent layer of Al₂O₃ that prevents further attack by oxygen, water or acids.

Anodising: Al is made the anode in electrolysis of dilute H₂SO₄ — a thicker, controlled Al₂O₃ layer is produced that can be dyed and is more protective. Used for aircraft parts and household items.
