Trends down Group 2

Atomic radius increases. IE₁ generally decreases. Reactivity increases. Melting points vary (not a simple trend due to crystal structures). All form 2+ ions only.

Solubility of hydroxides increases down the group: Be(OH)₂ insoluble → Mg(OH)₂ sparingly soluble → Ca(OH)₂ slightly soluble → Sr(OH)₂ and Ba(OH)₂ soluble.

Test for SO₄²⁻: add BaCl₂ solution (acidified with HCl) → white precipitate of BaSO₄ confirms sulfate. HCl prevents false positives from BaCO₃ or BaSO₃.

7.5

Thermal Stability of Carbonates and Nitrates

Group 2 carbonates decompose on heating: MCO₃ → MO + CO₂. Thermal stability increases down the group (BaCO₃ needs highest temperature). Smaller cations (higher charge density) polarise the carbonate ion more, weakening C–O bonds and lowering decomposition temperature.

CarbonateDecomposition temperature (°C)
MgCO₃~540
CaCO₃~840
SrCO₃~1290
BaCO₃~1360

Group 2 nitrates all decompose to oxide + NO₂ + O₂: 2M(NO₃)₂ → 2MO + 4NO₂ + O₂ (unlike Group 1 which form nitrites).

7.6

Industrial and Biological Uses

CompoundUse
CaO (quicklime)Steel manufacture (removes SiO₂ impurities); neutralising acidic soil
Ca(OH)₂ (slaked lime)Treating sewage, neutralising acid in soil, making cement/mortar
CaCO₃ (limestone)Building material, manufacture of glass and cement, blast furnace flux
MgSO₄ (Epsom salts)Laxative, bath salts, treating magnesium deficiency in plants
BaSO₄Barium meal for X-ray imaging of digestive system (opaque to X-rays, insoluble/non-toxic)
Mg (metal)Alloys for aircraft (light + strong); fireworks (bright white light)
